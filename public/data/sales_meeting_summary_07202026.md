## Executive Summary

The meeting focused on moving properties through construction faster, improving listing quality, strengthening communication with listing agents, and addressing homes that are receiving limited buyer activity.

The team reaffirmed that crews should concentrate on the properties closest to completion rather than spreading labor across multiple unfinished houses. Several homes are expected to be completed, photographed, or listed within the next one to two weeks.

A major operational change is that Brett will become the primary real estate liaison. He will inspect properties before listing, review listing photos, monitor buyer feedback, and help negotiate with agents on Chase's behalf.

## Major Decisions

### Listing and Sales Process

- Brett will inspect completed properties before they go active.
- Listing photographs should be sent to Brett for approval before being uploaded.
- Exterior photographs should be enhanced when shadows, trees, poor angles, or clutter make the property look worse than it does in person.
- Agents should avoid uploading unnecessary or unattractive photographs, including poor laundry-room, garage, or utility-area images.
- Brett should be added through ShowingTime where possible so he can receive showing notifications and feedback directly.
- The team decided not to request that Brett be added publicly as a co-listing agent because that could create tension with the existing agents.
- The property website should identify Brett as the real estate liaison and explain the listing-photo review process.
- Meeting notes, decisions, and supporting documents should be attached directly to each property record with timestamps.
- The website checklist should allow notes on individual tasks and link assignments to specific team members.

### Construction Management

- Crews should continue prioritizing the house closest to completion.
- Avoid having the same crew partially working on several properties at once.
- Countertops should be ordered immediately after cabinets are installed or confirmed ready.
- Appliances, lighting fixtures, windows, and other long-lead materials must be verified earlier.
- Final walkthroughs should occur before the contractor claims a house is complete.
- Landscaping, septic locations, drainage, odors, cleaning, and exterior appearance need to be addressed before listing whenever possible.

### Pricing and Market Strategy

- The first two weeks on the market are considered the most important period for generating interest.
- Properties with weak activity should be reviewed quickly for: poor listing photographs, landscaping or appearance issues, repeated negative feedback, odors or deferred repairs, and pricing adjustments.
- The team generally preferred correcting visible issues and improving presentation before making repeated price reductions.
- Future acquisitions should be approached cautiously because the market is currently moving slowly.
